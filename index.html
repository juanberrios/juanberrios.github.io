Profile

I'm a PhD candidate in the Department of Linguistics at the University of Pittsburgh. I'm broadly interested in the choices we make when speaking a language, and how learners acquire patterns of variation involving such choices. My research hence draws upon different subfields, including second language acquisition, sociolinguistics, and natural language processing. In my studies I make use of both experimental and large-scale corpus data, employ statistical models for analysis, and appeal to usage-based approaches to interpret findings. I am also an experienced language teacher and a practicing translator and interpreter. In my free time I enjoy watching movies, playing volleyball, and exploring Pittsburgh.

Recent work

Habitual and progressive aspect marking

In Spanish, speakers vary between single-word and multi-word units (e.g., estoy comiendo vs. como "I am eating") to express progressive and habitual aspectual functions. This study investigates this phenomenon in L2 Spanish, with a focus on the potential effects of lexical frequency on written production. The most recent version of this project was presented at the at the 8th Annual Symposium on Language Research at UC Davis.

Future-in-the-past expression

Two forms commonly used to express future in the past are the periphrastic future in the imperfect (e.g., iba a bailar ‘I was going to dance’) and the conditional (e.g., bailaría ‘I would dance’). In this study we investigate future-in-the-past expression in residents of Seville, Spain and Caracas, Venezuela using a preference task. This study was accepted for publication in John Benjamin's forthcoming Issues in Hispanic and Lusophone Linguistics (2022).

Morphological productivity in Spanish

A cross-dialectal analysis of the productivity (i.e., the extent to which a morphological pattern can be applied to new bases to form new words) of two competing Spanish diminutive suffixes (-ito, -illo). The most recent version of this project, with a focus on quantitative results, was presented at the AAAL 2022 conference.

Variable dequeísmo in the Spanish of Caracas

Using a corpus of contemporary Caracas Spanish we contribute a new analysis of dequeísmo; i.e., innovative use of de for postverbal complementation. Our analysis is the first to explore the influence of two usage-based factors, namely, lexical frequency and structural priming. We also included a random effect structure, also absent from research on dequeísmo. This study was published as a chapter of the Routledge Handbook of Variationist Approaches to Spanish (2021).
